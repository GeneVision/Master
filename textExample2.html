The McNeal surname The age-old Hebrides islands and the west coast of Scotland are the ancestral home of the McNeal family. Their name comes from the personal name Neil. The Gaelic form Mac Neill translates as son of Neil

Early Origin pt.1

The surname McNeal was first found in on the islands of Barra, Gigha, Colonsay, and Oronsay. According to traditional records in 1049, Niall, a direct descendent of King Niall of the Nine Hostages, landed in Barra and founded the Clan MacNeil of Barra.

Early Origins pt.2

However, another kinsman, some believe to be the younger brother of Niall named Anrothan, married a Princess of the Dalriadans, an ancient race from which sprang most of the early Scottish Kings.
